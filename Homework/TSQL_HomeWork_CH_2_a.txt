1.  ##### List the order of execution of a SQL query.
- *From, Where, Group by, Having Select, Order by.*
---
2.  ##### What does the from clause do?
- *First logically processed, specify the name of tables to query, and table operators.*
---
3.  ##### What does the where clause do?
- *You specify a predicate or logical expression to filter a row returned by the from phase. A predicate is an expression that is true or false.*
---
4.  ##### What does the group by clause do?
- *Arrange the row, returned by the previous logical query. Makes groups after it searches by rows.*
---
5.  ##### What does the having clause do?
- *Instead of the returning the rows Having clause is a group filter. only predicates evaluated to TRUE are returned.*
---
6.  ##### What does the select clause do?
- *specify the attributes(columns) for return.*
---
7.  ##### What does the distinct keyword do?
- *Remove duplicates, returns a rational result.*
---
8.  ##### What does the order by clause do?
- *Sort the rows for presentation purposes, very last to be processed.*
---
9.  ##### What does the limit clause do? This is not in the book.
- *Sets a limit on the returns.*
---
10. ##### What does the top clause do?
- *Is based on the number of rows filtered limit the number or percentage of rows. not standard, no skipping.*
---
11. ##### What do the offset . . . fetch . . . clauses do?
- *Supports skipping, extension of order by. how many rows to skip.*
